# Award Package Builder

Build a strong AF award or decoration narrative for James or a member of FD Flight.
Award cases live or die on quantified impact. Every claim needs a number tied to a mission outcome.

## Step 1: Gather Inputs
Ask for the following before drafting anything:

1. **Award type** — Individual decoration (AF Achievement, Commendation, MSM)? Performance award? Bonus/time-off award? EPR bullets?
2. **Who is this for?** — James, or a member of his flight? Name and rank.
3. **Rating period** — Start and end dates.
4. **Rating official** — Name and rank of the supervisor/approving official.
5. **Accomplishments** — List everything the member did. Raw notes are fine. Do not filter yet.

Then ask the hard questions — do not skip these:

- "What are the hard numbers? Training completion rates, personnel supported, schools executed, UTMs supervised, inspection results, error rates reduced, tickets closed, timelines met."
- "What problems did this person fix that nobody else was going to fix? What was the risk if they had not acted?"
- "What did leadership rely on this person to produce? Who used their work and for what decisions?"
- "What changed because of their actions? What is different now than before?"

Do not draft until you have at least 3 quantified data points. If the member cannot provide numbers, prompt: "Give me your best estimate and we will qualify it appropriately."

## Step 2: Build the Narrative

### For Decoration Citations (Achievement Medal, Commendation Medal, MSM)
Follow the AF decoration format. Lead with impact. No soft language.

**Structure:**
- Opening line: rank, name, organization, period of service covered
- Body paragraphs: 2-4 major accomplishments, each anchored to a number
- Closing line: reflection on character and potential

**Rules:**
- Every accomplishment gets a number: percentage, headcount, dollar value, time saved, or error rate
- Active voice throughout
- No: "assisted with," "helped to," "supported efforts to," "played a key role in"
- Yes: "led," "built," "executed," "reduced," "achieved," "delivered"
- Keep each accomplishment to 2-3 sentences: action, scope, result

### For EPR Bullets
Format: [Action verb]--[scope/context]--[quantified result]

Rules:
- One bullet per accomplishment
- Start with the strongest action verb
- Include numbers in every bullet where possible
- End with mission impact, not task completion
- Stay under the character limit for the EPR block

### For Bonus / Time-Off Award Cases
Build a memo or narrative that argues the case with evidence.

Structure:
1. What the member did (specific actions, not job description)
2. What the numbers show (quantified impact)
3. What would have happened without their actions (risk / counterfactual)
4. What the award ceiling is and why the ask is justified

## Step 3: Output

Present the draft with a note on any accomplishments that still lack quantification.
Flag soft language that needs strengthening before submission.

Save output to: active/drafts/award-[name]-[type]-draft.md
