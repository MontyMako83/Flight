# SOP Writer

Draft an Air Force SOP from James's inputs. Active voice. Numbered steps. No filler.

## Step 1: Gather Inputs
If James has not provided the following, ask for them before drafting:
1. **SOP title** — what process or procedure does this cover?
2. **Purpose** — what problem does this SOP solve? What does it enable?
3. **Scope** — who does this apply to? (All 301 FW members? FD Flight only? Specific AFSCs?)
4. **Key steps** — walk through the process. Rough notes are fine. James will refine.
5. **References** — any DAFIs, DAFMANs, PSDGs, or higher-level guidance this SOP implements?
6. **OPR** — who owns this SOP?

Do not draft until you have at minimum: title, purpose, and key steps.

## Step 2: Draft the SOP

Use the following structure. Every section uses active voice and numbered steps.
No passive constructions. No filler language.

---

# [SOP TITLE]
**OPR**: [Office of Primary Responsibility]
**Effective Date**: [Date]
**Supersedes**: [Previous version if applicable, or N/A]

---

## 1. PURPOSE
[One to three sentences. State what this SOP covers and why it exists.
Lead with the mission impact. Example: "This SOP establishes procedures for...
to ensure 301 FW members receive accurate and timely..."]

## 2. REFERENCES
[List applicable DAFIs, DAFMANs, PSDGs, instructions, and policy letters.
Format: Document number — Title]

## 3. APPLICABILITY
[Who this applies to. Be specific: rank, AFSC, unit, or role.]

## 4. RESPONSIBILITIES

### 4.1 [Role/Position]
[Bullet list of responsibilities for this role]

### 4.2 [Role/Position]
[Bullet list of responsibilities]

[Add sections for each responsible party]

## 5. PROCEDURES

### 5.1 [Phase or Sub-Process Name]
1. [Step one — action verb, specific actor, clear action]
2. [Step two]
3. [Step three]
[Continue as needed]

### 5.2 [Next Phase]
1. [Step]
2. [Step]

[Continue for all phases of the process]

## 6. RECORDS
[What documentation is created, where it is stored, and retention requirements.
Reference AFI 33-322 / AF RDS as applicable.]

## 7. COMMON ERRORS
[Optional but recommended. List the top 2-4 mistakes that happen with this process
and how to avoid them.]

---

## Step 3: Review Prompt
After drafting, ask James:
"Draft complete. Review for accuracy, missing steps, or policy conflicts.
Flag anything that needs a regulation cite."

Save output to: active/drafts/[sop-title]-draft.md
